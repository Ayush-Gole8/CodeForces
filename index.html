The Exact Problem We Solve

Not "Lack of Information" ‚Äî It's "Orchestration Failure"

The SPIT problem statement identifies the real issue: millions become "unwitting logistics managers" piecing together journeys from disconnected sources. The core pathology isn't information scarcity‚Äîit's that metro, bus, and auto-rickshaw systems operate independently without coordination. This creates systemic uncertainty that individual operators cannot resolve.

"Commuters often discover problems only after they are already committed to a route. They may find themselves standing at the wrong platform, waiting for a service that has changed, or struggling to find a reliable last mile option."

The Decision Fatigue Problem

Commuters make constant decisions under incomplete information, becoming de facto logistics managers for their own journeys.

  • When to leave home without knowing platform conditions
  • Which train to catch without transfer certainty
  • Whether to wait for a bus or seek alternatives
  • How to manage the final stretch with unpredictable last-mile options

The Systemic Coordination Gap

Transport modes function in silos, offering only partial journey visibility while operators lack awareness of intersecting systems.

  • Metro doesn't know if connecting buses are delayed
  • Bus operators can't anticipate platform overcrowding
  • Auto-rickshaw drivers circle without knowing where demand will emerge
  • City planners lack cohesive data on where transfers actually fail

How We Achieve This: The Technical Architecture

Three Systems Working as One Nervous System

This is the complete architecture that transforms the concept into a city-scale solution addressing each aspect of the SPIT problem.

1
Pillar One

Friction Sensing Network

Addresses: "The lack of timely and end to end visibility makes it difficult to anticipate issues or adapt smoothly."

We deploy sensors at friction points (platform entrances, transfer zones, auto stands) to measure what matters: crowd density, wait times, and abandonment rates‚Äînot just vehicle locations. This creates the visibility the problem statement says is missing.

Direct Problem Tackle

Instead of giving commuters partial views from each transport mode (the core problem), we create a unified view of system stress points. Operators finally see how their systems intersect with others.

2
Pillar Two

Anticipatory Nudge Engine

Addresses: "Commuters must decide when to leave, which train to catch, whether the bus will arrive... under incomplete information."

ML models predict transfer failures 15-30 minutes ahead. When probability exceeds threshold, system sends single-choice SMS nudges: "Take 7:45 metro instead‚ÄîPlatform 2 overcrowding predicted."

Direct Problem Tackle

We eliminate decision-making under uncertainty by providing the one optimal adjustment. Commuters stop being logistics managers and become passengers again.

3
Pillar Three

Collective Rebalancing Loop

Addresses: "Transport services operate with limited awareness of how their systems intersect with others... some routes are overcrowded while others remain underutilized."

Prevents the "everyone follows the same app" problem. When a nudged alternate route exceeds 60% capacity, system automatically diversifies subsequent nudges. This coordinates collective flow.

Direct Problem Tackle

We create the awareness between systems that the problem statement says is missing. The network self-balances, utilizing all available capacity instead of overloading "optimal" routes.
